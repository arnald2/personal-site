1990

Cuba

Born in Cuba during the "Special Period" of scarcity. We ate rice, climbed trees, played hide-and-seek in the darkness of daily blackouts. 90s Cuban life was not great for most adults, but not terrible for kids. At least not for a kid that only wanted to eat rice and fried eggs every meal.
2001

Welcome to Miami, Bienvenidos a Miami

Landed in the U.S. expecting everyone to live in the Home Alone mansion. That was not quite the case, but with the shock of getting access to video games, blockbuster video, air conditioning and hot showers, it took me 10 years to notice.
2008

Starting College

Graduated high school, started college and got my first car: a 2000 Mitsubishi Mirage. It blew a tire faithfully every week, but it was freedom on wheels.
2011

Loosing STEM

Changed majors to Computer Engineering and took 18 credits of advanced math and weed out classes. Failed miserably, but finally learned that "studying" does not just mean reviewing notes 2 hours before the exam. Also started doing 12 credits per semester. Things worked out pretty well after that.
2013

Adulting

Moved to D.C. for my first full time job. Quickly realized i was not built for real winters, rent is expensive, and doing dishes is no fun. Anyways... best 4 years of my life.
2017

California Life

Moved to Los Gatos to join Amazon. My apartment walls were literally removed while I lived there, and maintenance people would come unannounced into my apartment, but hey, the trails and wineries of the Santa Cruz mountains were stunning.
2018

I Do

Got married in New Jersey. We honeymooned in Moorea, where I faced off against the natural predator of Cubans (sharks)... luckily for me, they were little.
2019

Jersey Life

Moved back to the East Coast (Jersey City). Traded the California weather for the NYC hustle and bustle, the Comedy Cellar, and the PATH train daily mosh pit.
2020

Mastered It

Finally finished my Master's in CS. Spent five years of weekends and late nights, for a degree I have not used much professionally. But I did learn a lot and really enjoyed most classes and projects.
2021

New Dad

Became a father. The hospital letting us take the baby home with zero supervision, after 48 hours of not sleeping felt like a massive oversight on their part at the time. But it all worked out, baby is still around.
2024

Round Two

Welcomed baby girl #2. Twice the chaos and half the sleep, but can't imagine life without those two rowdy girls.
2026

Right Now

The present day. Trying to enjoy the little moments, while the world entropy index keeps increasing exponentially.
